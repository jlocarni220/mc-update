Cohort Analysis
Where Revenue Actually Comes From

We ran a full cohort analysis across all 17,454 customers and 39,000+ orders. Two things jumped out.

First: our power users drive the business. Just 2.4% of customers (421 people with 13+ orders) generate over half of all lifetime revenue. These are our subscribers and superfans — the clearest proof that the product creates genuine habitual behavior.

Revenue by Customer Tier
2.4% of customers drive 51% of revenue

Second: repeat customers come back fast. Median time to second purchase is just 32 days. 44% of all repeat customers reorder within 30 days. Over 70% come back within 60 days.

Time to Second Purchase
44% reorder within 30 days · 71% within 60 days

Why this matters: A tight reorder window means our retention interventions (email flows, subscription nudges, reorder reminders) hit during peak intent. Every improvement in 30-day conversion compounds across the entire customer base.

Early 2025 cohorts are already showing $218–$275 in lifetime value per customer with months of maturity ahead. Our top 10% of customers drive 50% of all revenue — healthy concentration that reflects genuine product love, not promotional dependency.

Acquisition
Paid Media

$857K total ad spend across Meta and Google. 10,220 new DTC customers. Blended nCAC of $84.

New Customer Acquisition Cost (nCAC)
H1 avg: $60 · H2 avg: $116

H1 was efficient ($60 avg nCAC). H2 costs rose as we scaled Meta and hit diminishing returns. A few things happened in response:

  • Pulled back Meta spend in Q4 — from $99K/month (Sep) to $9K (Dec) — while testing new creative and audiences
  • Starting October, a portion of Meta spend shifted to driving retail traffic at key wholesale accounts (Total Wine, Lowes Foods, etc.) — so the dip in DTC efficiency is partly by design
  • Google was consistent all year — 5.2x average ROAS from February onward, delivering customers at a fraction of Meta's cost

As wholesale scales, company-wide acquisition economics improve structurally — more on that below.

Distribution
Wholesale — The Chapter That's Just Beginning

$396K in 2025 wholesale revenue, +227% YoY. The full-year number undersells the trajectory.

Q4 2024
Launched DTC first — building direct relationships with end consumers before entering retail.
Q1 2025
Beta-tested retail via Airgoods and self-distribution. All SKUs ranked #1 and #2 on Airgoods platform-wide, across all categories. Launched into Total Wine across all Arizona locations.
April 2025
Arizona banned hemp-derived THC beverages. Lost our earliest wholesale market. Pivoted — signed DSD partners in TN (Empire), NC (Tryon), FL (Cavalier), and TX (Dynamo).
Q4 2025
Launched DSD in Tennessee and North Carolina. Chain placements in Total Wine, Lowes Foods, and The Fresh Market.
January 2026 — Sprouts Farmers Market
Launched into 120 Sprouts locations across Texas and Florida — one of just 7 THC beverage brands selected. The largest grocery launch of THC products to date. January wholesale: $135K — 34% of total 2025 wholesale, in one month.

Reflections
What 2025 Taught Us
  • Retention is the moat. 65% repeat revenue, 31% lifetime repeat rate, 32-day median reorder cycle. The product does the selling.
  • DTC alone won't get us to scale. Paid acquisition gets more expensive in a regulated category. Wholesale is the unlock — 2025 built the infrastructure.
  • Subscription economics are improving structurally. Churn declining, cycle length doubling, subscriber count growing — all at once.
  • Regulatory volatility is real, but we're built for it. Arizona's ban cost us our first market. Our low-dose formulation (2mg THC) survives where high-dose competitors get pushed out. Sprouts selecting us as 1 of 7 validates this.
Ahead
2026 Priorities
  • Go deeper in Texas and Florida. 120 Sprouts stores live. DSD partners in place to add retail doors beyond Sprouts.
  • Continue building TN and NC. Total Wine, Lowes Foods, The Fresh Market are live. Expanding within chains and adding new ones.
  • Optimize DTC acquisition. Rebalancing toward Google, scaling email/SMS, improving subscription conversion.
